Introduction

The blue butterfly is one of nature’s most enchanting creatures, admired for its vivid coloration and delicate beauty. Found in various parts of the world, these butterflies captivate onlookers with their shimmering blue wings and graceful flight.Blue butterflies play a crucial role in ecosystems as pollinators and indicators of environmental health. For instance, the Chalkhill Blue (Lysandra coridon) has shown variations in chromosome numbers across Europe, providing insights into evolutionary processes and the impacts of climate change.
